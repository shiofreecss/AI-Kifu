Games by Honinbo Jowa

Below a collection of 293 games by Honinbo Jowa (1787-1847).

Such collections can be found in print, or digitally various places on the net. For example on jakkoji (275, html), on flygo (282, zip with 263+33 sgfs), and on lifein19x19 (292, pdf). The above link points to a tar file with 297 sgfs, but actually contains only 293 games, since the four pairs 103.sgf, 106.sgf / 107.sgf, 135.sgf / 185.sgf, 186.sgf / 268.sgf, 269.sgf contain different versions of the same game.

Name

One finds the names Todani (or Toya) Matsunosuke, Kadono (or Kuzuno) Matsunosuke, Kadono Jowa, Honinbo Jowa. For details on Jowa's name and origin, see John Fairbairn's note in rec.games.go.

Rank

Kadono (or Kuzuno) Jowa became the 12th Honinbo in 1827, and Meijin-Godokoro in 1831. Rank progression: 1d around 1802, 2d around 1807, 5d: 1815, 6d: 1819, 7d: 1827, 8d: 1828, Meijin: 1831.

The 21-game match with Nagasaka Inosuke

From July 1807 to June 1808, the future Jowa played a 21-game match against Nagasaka Inosuke. At that time Jowa was 4d, and Nagasaka, officially 2d, was considered to be 6d. The match started on josen, with Nagasaka taking W in each game. After 12 games Jowa led 8-4, and they continued on sen-ai-sen, with Nagasaka taking W two out of every three games. In the following nine games Jowa scored 6-3. [SL]
